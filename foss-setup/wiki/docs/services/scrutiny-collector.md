## About

scrutiny-collector is the collector-only half of the Scrutiny SMART-health stack (glue-10) ‚Äî it runs on both `mini` (`foss-setup/configs/docker-stack/stacks/scrutiny-collector/compose.yaml`) and `rig` (`foss-setup/configs/host/rig/scrutiny-collector/compose.yaml`), reads the local disks' SMART data with a bundled `smartctl`, and POSTs it to the Scrutiny hub (the `scrutiny` omnibus on the NAS at `http://192.168.10.4:8080`). It has **no web UI** ‚Äî the dashboard is the hub. The image is `ghcr.io/analogj/scrutiny` master-collector (digest-pinned). It needs `cap_add: SYS_RAWIO` plus a `/run/udev` mount and the raw disk devices passed in. On the mini that is the single SATA SSD (`/dev/sda`); on the rig NVMe drives additionally require `cap_add: SYS_ADMIN` and the **controller** nodes (`/dev/nvme0`, not the `nvme0n1` namespace), and the iPod at `/dev/sda` is deliberately excluded. The endpoint is the NAS **IP** (not a hostname) to dodge the container-DNS quirk (net-16). It sweeps daily at 06:00 (`COLLECTOR_CRON_SCHEDULE`); `COLLECTOR_HOST_ID` tags the host so the hub groups disks by machine.

## Troubleshooting

- **On the rig, NVMe drives are not detected by the collector.** ‚Äî NVMe needs both `SYS_RAWIO` and `SYS_ADMIN`, and you must pass the controller node `/dev/nvmeN` (e.g. `/dev/nvme0`), NOT the `/dev/nvme0n1` namespace. Verify inside the container: `docker exec scrutiny-collector smartctl --scan` should list each `/dev/nvmeN -d nvme`. Fix the `cap_add`/`devices` in the rig `compose.yaml` and `docker compose up -d`.
- **The collector runs but nothing shows up on the hub dashboard for this host.** ‚Äî The collector cannot reach the hub. It POSTs to `COLLECTOR_API_ENDPOINT=http://192.168.10.4:8080` ‚Äî confirm reachability from the container's host (`curl -s http://192.168.10.4:8080/api/health`) and that the NAS `scrutiny` container is up. Then force a run: `docker exec scrutiny-collector /opt/scrutiny/bin/scrutiny-collector-metrics run`.
