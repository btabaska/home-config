Somewhere along the way

We stopped owning our things. We started renting them back from the people who made them. Your photos live on someone else’s computer. Your music disappears when a license expires. Your movies vanish when a price triples. You pay, every month, forever — for the privilege of borrowing your own memories.

We thought a computer should do the opposite. It should give you something and then get out of the way. So we built one that does.

The idea

It just works.
And it’s yours.

Those two ideas were never supposed to be in tension. A thing can be effortless and owned. Magical and private. Simple on the outside, completely yours on the inside. Hearth is one box — well, a few quiet ones — that takes back every part of your digital life and hands it to you whole. You don’t manage it. You just live with it.

Play

Console-grade power, summoned on demand.

There’s a serious machine in the office — and Hearth streams it to any TV, laptop or tablet in the house at full quality, with no console to buy and no cloud subscription to rent. It wakes when you press play and sleeps when you’re done. And when friends want to play, you host the world for them — Minecraft, Valheim, the old favorites — on a server that’s genuinely yours.

  • Your rig, on every screen. Wake-on-LAN, stream, suspend — automatically.
  • Host for friends. Persistent worlds, your rules, your save files.
  • No idle cost. The power only spins up when you actually play.

Privacy

It isn’t a feature.
It’s the foundation.

Most companies bolt privacy on at the end and put it in the ads. We started here and built outward. When the computer lives in your house and answers only to you, privacy isn’t a promise anyone has to keep. It’s just how the thing is shaped.

🛡️ Zoned by design

Your devices, your work laptop, the cameras, and every chatty smart-gadget live in separate, walled-off lanes. A compromised lightbulb can’t see your photos. That’s enforced at the front door of the network.

🚫 Nothing phones home

Ads and trackers are filtered for every device in the house before they ever load. The default isn’t “collect everything.” The default is silence.

🔑 The keys are yours

Your passwords, your encryption, your backups — held by you. No master key sits on a server somewhere waiting to be subpoenaed or breached.
